WMAA 10th Grade Honors English Project

"Faces in Motion"

Faces in Motion

is a project by Mr. Williams' sophomore honors English students. It is inspired by John Steinbeck's The Grapes of Wrath. In this novel, a family named the Joads travel from their home in Oklahoma to California looking work during the Great Depression. The novel documents the family's struggles in their migration. The Joads are just one family effected during the Great Depression, but they represent many in similar conditions. Mr. Williams' second hour and sixth hour classes were inspired and to learn more about the global issue they created this massive, 8-foot by 16-foot world map. The background is a compilation of photos of people and events around the world. The strings show the movement of people and their color represents the continent that the people are coming from which you can find in the key below. QR codes are placed throughout the project to provide an efficient way for you to access our links that contain more information about specific topics. Read the paragraph below on how to use QR codes if you are uncertain about how to use them. Our project is fully student created and fully student funded through donations and fundraising. We hope you enjoy our project as much as we enjoyed making it!
