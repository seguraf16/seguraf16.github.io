Central Africa Migration

Migrations happen all throughout the world, and it is seen in history continuing to the present day. One region in particular that has produced many migrants is Central Africa. Central Africa is a region that is decimated by internal conflicts. The countries that were paid with particular attention were the Congo, Chad, Rwanda, and Sudan. Over 550,000 people have been displaced in Central Africa, and about 350,000 Central Africans have fled to neighboring countries due to the humanitarian crises, genocide, and the lack of resources for its citizens. Rwanda

has had a history of violence. In 1992, Rwanda was hit with a famine that caused the people to move to neighboring countries. By the end of 1992 there were 85,800 people in Uganda that were native to Rwanda. In 1994, a civil war and genocide broke out and caused 7.5 million Rwandan people to flee their homes. In 2012, the U.N. forced the Rwandans that found asylum in Uganda to return home. Today the Immigration rate of Rwanda 62.8 per 1000 people.

Congo

In 1994 The Rwandan genocide has taken full affecting the migration of hundreds of Rwandaises people to move into the Congo. The recognised the problem and establish 2 million huts for refugees from the genisode. 1996 the neighboring countries invade the congo to root out the leftover perpetrators of the genocide left hiding in the Congo. In 2015 The Congo has mostly achieved peace the central government has slowly regained its power. Its relations with its neighboring countries have not gotten any better.
