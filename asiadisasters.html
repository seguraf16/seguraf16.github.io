Maldives Islands

Maldives Islands: Another form of a natural disasters is the sea level rising. The Maldives islands are located off the coast of India. There are 1,200 islands that are currently under the threat of being sunk by the rising sea level. The islands themselves do not rise very high over the sea level, the highest point being 8 feet above. The cause of the sinking of the islands is due to the melting polar ice caps which also increases the amount of fresh water. As the islands continue to be sunken by the rise of sea level , there has been arrangements made for evacuation when they become inhabitable. Evacuees of the islands will evacuate to Australia, India, and Sri Lanka where they will receive proper aid.

China

42 million please had to leave their homes due to natural disasters in 2010. Most being climate refugees who are forced to move from their homes due to climate change. For example; tsunamis, earthquakes and even global warming. Some climate refugees move form urban areas or areas of distraction to inner cities to escape the climate.

Philippines

In the year 2013, Typhoon Haiyan hit the Phillipeans leaving about four million overall displaced and at least 10,000 casualties in the city. With winds reaching 195 m/h and gusts of 235 m/h, major damage was caused to the islands leaving schools, hospitals, offices and market places is destruction. The 7.2 magnitude earthquake that stuck Bohol Province added considerable damage as well. Refugees took shelter in neighboring cities that weren't as effected by the disasters, such as Leyte. eastern Samar, Western and Central Visayas, and the Bicol and Northern Mindanao relations.
