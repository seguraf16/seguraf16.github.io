Natural Disasters in the Western World

- There have been 69,098 deaths per year since 2001 due to natural disasters.

- 10,000 people die every year due to earthquakes

- In 2010 Haiti experienced an earthquake killing 316,00 people.

- Hawaii experiences more than one tsunami a year.

- In the last 10 years over 2.6 million people were affected by natural disasters.

- Hurricanes travel with winds at 74 miles per hour or greater.

Since 2012, the Western Hemisphere has experienced a plethora natural disasters ranging from earthquakes to Hurricanes. One of the more notable disasters was hurricane Sandy which displaced over 1 million people along the east coast of North and South America. According to an IDMC study, an estimated 746,000 citizens of the United States were displaced and another 343,000 from Cuba. With the greatest number of those displaced in 2013 in the Western Hemisphere, the Oklahoma tornado streak displaced more than 10,000 people. A relatively stable year, 2014 thankfully saw very little of Mother Nature's capabilities. These only being the more disastrous events, many have occurred displacing millions worldwide. For years these unavoidable disasters have kicked out multitudes of diversities from their homes. It's truly hard to comprehend the number of lives affected by these disasters. Imagine Wrigley Field at its maximum capacity(about 41 thousand). A full baseball stadium of people forced out of their homes to face God knows what. Now imagine that one event, one disaster does this to 20 stadiums of people.
