Mediterranean Migrant Frenzy

Rosita Boland: ‘I watched as migrants from Syria, Bangladesh, Mali and Nigeria took their first steps on land since embarking from Libya on boats they knew could as easily transform overnight into their coffins.’

Most migrants come from West Africa as well as Somalia, and Syria. Libya is the country of transit. There was more than 10,000 migrants arriving in the beginning of 2015. 2,000 of those migrants were rescued in April in Sicily at sea. Europe faces difficult challenges facing migrants. There are multitudes that increase in size of migrants crossing the Mediterranean when seasons change to better temperatures. Still the death toll increases inevitably, but now Europe is in a frenzy whether to provide operations for finding missing migrants at sea that are trying to come in their country.

Facts

- 22,000 people have died crossing the Mediterranean since the year 2000

- Recently some trying to pass the Mediterranean paid a fee of $700+ per person

- Foreign minister says 90% of rescue effort has fallen on Italian navy

- The death toll for crossing this year is 909. In comparison, the death toll was 50 during this same time frame

- 2014: 55.3 deaths per 500

- Q1 2015: 46.5 deaths per 100

- A boat capsized on the 19th that was 20 meters long and carrying 900 passengers

- 388% increase in illegal crossings into Italy and Malta between 2012 and 2013

"If I stayed Syria, I would have to be part of one party or of ISIS. I'd have to take up arms and kill people. But I don't want that. I only want to take care of my children,"- Mahmoud Shubat, a Syrian construction worker who fled to Libya with final destination to Italy.
