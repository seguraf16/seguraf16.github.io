Border Patrol

The Mexican Border Patrol is one of the biggest border patrols in the entire world. With over 21,000 agents it is one of the largest law agencies in the entire world. The border patrol deports over 400,000 immigrants per year and most of those are Mexicans. The border control spans over 1989 miles which is just a short 462 miles shorter than the infamous Route 66. All of these people however were not hired on and put on the border for waste. They have a mission that is very important and has meaning. The border patrols mission is to prevent terrorists or terrorist weapons from entering the United States. This is because of the September 11 events, which changed the way of life throughout all of America. One of the biggest things that the border patrol does is to prevent illegal drug activities across the border, which is a large part of Mexican immigration. The border patrol is an important part of the Mexican immigration cylcle and affects greatly this nation.

Naturalization

In order to reside here in the United States, you need a green card to work. People can also get into the United States through asylum (political asylum or religious asylum). There is also a naturalization test to become a citizen. This is not an easily accomplished task, due to most U.S. Citizens voting to make it a difficult task (to help restrict unnecessary amounts of migrants). Another aspect of the struggle to become a U.S. Citizen is in the process of border control.
